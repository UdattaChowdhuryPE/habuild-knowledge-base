# Habuild HR Policy Manual

## Leave Policy

### Casual Leave

Eligible employees are entitled to 12 casual leaves per calendar year. Casual leaves are intended for personal emergencies, health issues, or urgent personal matters.

Eligibility:
- Full-time employees with at least 3 months of service
- Pro-rata for new hires in their first year

Application:
- Must be requested at least 24 hours in advance
- Requests submitted via HR portal or email to hr@habuild.in
- Approval is subject to business needs

Carryforward:
- Unused casual leaves do not carry forward to the next year
- Maximum 2 leaves can be accumulated if unused by quarter-end

### Sick Leave

Sick leaves are provided for medical emergencies and health-related absences. Employees are entitled to 10 paid sick leaves per calendar year.

Medical Certificate Requirement:
- For absences of 3 consecutive days or more, a medical certificate is mandatory
- Certificate must be issued by a registered medical practitioner
- Should be submitted within 2 working days of return

Approval Process:
- Same-day application allowed if emergent
- Retrospective applications allowed for unexpected illnesses
- HR reviews and approves applications within 24 hours

Documentation:
- Medical certificate must be in original or attested copy
- Digital copies accepted via secure email

### Leave Without Pay (LWP)

Employees may request LWP for personal reasons beyond other leave categories. LWP is unpaid and should be approved by their manager and HR.

Eligibility:
- Available after all paid leaves are exhausted
- Limited to 10 days per calendar year
- Not applicable during probation period (first 6 months)

### Notice Period Policy

Notice period is the advance notification required for resignation or involuntary separation.

Standard Notice Periods:
- Junior Level (0-3 years): 15 days
- Senior Level (3-7 years): 30 days
- Management Level (7+ years): 60 days

During Notice Period:
- Employee remains employed and receives full salary
- All benefits continue as normal
- Employee is expected to complete handover tasks
- Non-compete clause becomes effective upon resignation

Waiver:
- Notice period can be waived at management discretion
- Waiver is not a right and depends on business requirements

## Medical Benefits

All full-time employees and their dependents are covered under the company health insurance plan.

Coverage:
- In-patient hospitalization
- Out-patient consultations (up to Rs. 5,000 per year)
- Preventive health checkups (annual)
- Maternity benefits

Copay:
- 20% copay for non-network hospitals
- Zero copay for network hospitals

## Grievance Redressal

Employees can file grievances through the HR portal or directly with the HR department.

Process:
1. Submit written grievance within 10 days of the incident
2. HR acknowledges receipt within 2 working days
3. Investigation completed within 15 days
4. Resolution communicated in writing

Confidentiality:
- All grievances are treated confidentially
- No retaliation against employees filing genuine grievances
